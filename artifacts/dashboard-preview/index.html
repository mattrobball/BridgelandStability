Prop 2.4 Existence of HN filtrations ▲Stronger hypotheses

A stability function on an abelian category admits HN filtrations whenever a suitable chain condition holds on subobjects and quotient objects, phrased in terms of phase-monotone chains.

!
The paper uses phase-ordered chain conditions (no infinite subobject chains of strictly increasing phase, no infinite quotient chains of strictly decreasing phase). The Lean proof assumes every object has a finite subobject lattice — strictly stronger, and it excludes e.g. coherent sheaves on curves, which do satisfy the paper's hypothesis. A faithful version would need custom predicates for the phase-ordered chain conditions.

§5

Stability conditions on triangulated categories

A stability condition is a slicing plus a compatible central charge; equivalently, a t-structure whose heart carries a stability function with HN property.

Def 5.1 Stability condition ●Complete

A stability condition on a triangulated category 𝒟 is a slicing together with a compatible central charge Z : K(𝒟) → ℂ, i.e. for every semistable object E of phase φ one has Z(E) ∈ ℝ₊ · exp(iπφ).

!
Formalized in the class-map variant, which is strictly more general than the paper's K-theoretic statement and specializes back to it.

§6

The space of stability conditions

A topology on the set of locally finite stability conditions, and the proof that forgetting the slicing is a local homeomorphism onto its image.

Lemma 6.1 Generalized metric on slicings ◐Partial

The space of slicings carries a left-invariant generalized metric measuring how far one slicing has to be rotated and stretched to contain another.

!
The Lean instance provides a pseudo-extended-metric space using the supremum of phase differences. The paper defines the distance as the infimum of containment radii. The two formulations are equivalent, but that equivalence is not yet proved in Lean — hence no separation (d = 0 → P = Q) and no upgrade to a generalized metric.

Deformation of stability conditions

Small perturbations of the central charge can be lifted to genuine stability conditions under local finiteness.

Thm 7.1 Deformation of stability conditions ◆Generalized

Starting from a locally finite stability condition σ, any small perturbation of its central charge can be lifted uniquely to a genuine stability condition close to σ.

!
The Lean statement takes the neighbourhood radius ε₀ as an explicit parameter. The paper's Theorem 7.1 instead produces ε₀ existentially from local finiteness. The existing proof is strictly stronger in the sense that it covers every valid ε₀, so recovering the paper statement is a packaging step.
